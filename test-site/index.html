Rock Climbing: Physical Problem Solving

Rock climbing accross the board is a challenging sport and is known to be pretty demanding physically. To be able to climb grand walls takes strength in the following areas:

  • Arms
  • Fingers
  • Abs
  • Legs

However, just as much as the sport is physically challenging, it is just as much a brain gain as well.

Most new climbers understand the physical requirements of the sport, even if they dont meet some of them initially. How, then, can some people who do not appear to be wildly strong climb up huge walls without assistance from ropes and gear? Turns out, most of them are pretty good problem solvers.

Climbing is a physical sport but using your head could make a world of difference. For example, one problem new climbers normally face is a hand hold being just out of reach. Possible solutions? One way to solve this is to give it a jump and hope to snag the hold before falling. Though this could work, a better solution would be to find a higher foothold, instead, and then standing up to the next hold. Another common problem accross climbers of all levels is arm fatigue. Besides just getting stronger, a way to answer this problme is to use less muscles while you climb. But, how?! Well typically, this means using long arms to give the back muscles and upper arms a little rest while you climbing. Using legs to push is also a very good solution to arm and upper body fatigue.

Rock climbing is a great sport! It is a fun alternative to weight-lifting but with an added mind game in order to answer physical problems.

If you have any interest in climbing feel free to check out Texas Rock Gym by making a reservation. Don't forget to fill out the waiver online before you come to save time!
